Frequently asked

Questions, answered

PeriTrace requires an iPhone that supports ARKit.

iPhone models with LiDAR are recommended for a smoother experience and more precise captures.

PeriTrace supports iOS 18.6 and later.

PeriTrace organizes your work by pattern. Each pattern is a set that can hold up to 40 pattern pieces, which keeps the app quick and responsive. If your physical pattern has more pieces than that, split it across multiple patterns in the app.

From the Home screen:

  • Add a new pattern — tap the Add New button at the bottom of the screen.
  • Rename or delete — swipe a pattern left or right to reveal the Rename and Delete buttons.
  • Rearrange the list — long-press a pattern and drag it to a new position.
  • Open a pattern — tap it to enter its canvas, where its pieces live.

Open a pattern from the Home screen, then tap Trace at the bottom right to open the AR camera (you may be asked to allow camera access). PeriTrace traces one piece per camera session.

In the camera view, move your phone slowly back and forth over a flat surface until the reticle sits flush and steady — that's your cue that you're ready to trace.

Two tracing modes:

  • Vertex tracing — aim the reticle at the outline or seam line and tap Trace Vertices to place points one by one. Points connect automatically, and a dashed line closes the shape between your first and last point — no need to close it yourself.
  • Marking — aim at a mark on the physical piece and tap Mark. Marks stand alone and never connect to vertices or other marks.
Contour detection. Point the camera at a piece and long-press the screen for at least half a second to auto-detect its outline. Moving your finger or releasing early cancels it. It reads one outline at a time, and works best when the piece contrasts clearly with the surface beneath it — a light piece on a dark surface, or vice versa.

Getting a clean capture: you don't need to hold the phone perfectly perpendicular — PeriTrace's AR tracking handles some angle. Phones without LiDAR are more sensitive to calibration, so keep the phone 0.5–2 meters above the pattern and make sure the reticle is sitting flat on the surface before you start.

Finishing up: tap Done to save the piece to the canvas (it needs at least three points first), or Dismiss to discard it and return to the canvas.

The canvas is a vector workspace with full gesture support, and your layout is preserved automatically when you export.

  • Zoom & pan — pinch and drag to move around the workspace.
  • Move & rotate a piece — drag to reposition, or use two fingers to rotate.
  • Delete a piece — long-press it, then tap Delete in the popup.
Pro tip for tricky pieces. If you're not confident in a trace, capture the same piece a few times and stack the results on the canvas — the overlap shows you the average outline.

A canvas holds up to 40 pieces per pattern; split larger physical patterns across multiple patterns in the app.

Exports keep your canvas arrangement exactly as you left it, at true 1:1 scale.

  • Tap Export at the bottom left of the canvas — it's enabled once there's at least one piece on it.
  • Choose a format, then pick a destination from the standard iOS share sheet.
  • Regardless of what you choose, PeriTrace also saves a backup to On My iPhone → PeriTrace → Exports.
  • Tap Quick Look right after exporting to preview the file instantly.
PDFUniversal layout format — great for printing, vector tools, or apparel CAD.
